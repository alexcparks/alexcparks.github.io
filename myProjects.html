My Work

Over several years at Queen's University, I had the opportunity to learn and study a variety of subjects and work with dozens of individuals, all sharing the same passion for engineering. In that time, I have acquired and refined many report writing skills, learned several coding languages, and, most importantly, learned to apply problem solving skills thtough the engineering process.

Below are a few of my reports, projects and labs, sample of the report skills that I have acquired during my studies and as an introduction to my writing and organization style.

Gear-Box Design Challenge

One of my favourites, yet one of the most challenging projects I have done during my studies at Queen's. The team was tasked with designing a gearbox for a miniature electric vehicle to maximize performance in a hill climb and top-speed event. This project is a great example of my passion for design work and engineering.

Noise Control Project

The project consisted of a challenge to reduce the sound of a specific instrument in a real machine shop by using experimental data, proposing a design, and then justifying it. I enjoyed this project as it was a very open-ended and problem with very little information given.

IC Engine Lab

A lab to determine the efficiency of a specific internal combustion engine through several experimental data sets. To obtain the variation in the data, the engine was run under several different loads, and the resultant properties at each engine speed were compared and discussed. The breakdown of the data and this report can be used for calibration and tuning of this specific engine.
